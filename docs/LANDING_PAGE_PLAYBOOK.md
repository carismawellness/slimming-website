# High-Converting Landing Page Playbook — Carisma Slimming

Every offer or package landing page **must** include the six elements below.
Each is framed for a medical slimming clinic and aligned with the compassionate,
evidence-led brand voice (see `BRAND_VOICE.md`). Treat this as a non-negotiable
checklist before any offer page goes live.

---

## 1. Offer Name in the H1 Tag

Use a single `<h1>` per page and make it the offer name — never the brand name or
a generic slogan. Include "Malta" where it reads naturally (e.g. "Medically
Supervised Slimming Program in Malta") so the page earns local SEO relevance
without keyword-stuffing. The H1 should name what the visitor gets, not what we
want from them, keeping the tone calm and concrete rather than hype-driven. Every
other heading on the page drops to `<h2>` or lower to preserve a clean hierarchy.

## 2. Clearly List the Package Contents

Spell out exactly what is included so there are no surprises: number of
consultations and follow-up sessions, any medications or treatments (with the
fact that they are doctor-prescribed and supervised), the cadence of check-ins,
nutrition and movement planning, and any support between visits. Use plain
language and a scannable list rather than dense paragraphs. Transparency here
builds the "safety" the brand voice promises — the reader should finish this
section knowing precisely what they are investing in.

## 3. Video of the Practitioner Explaining the Package

Feature a short video of the doctor or lead practitioner explaining the program
in their own words. Doctor-led trust is our biggest differentiator in a market
full of crash diets and influencer fixes, so put a real, credentialed human on
camera. Keep it warm and educational — what the program does, who it is for, and
what to realistically expect — never salesy or over-promising. Place it high
enough on the page that visitors meet the person responsible for their care early.

## 4. Lots of Before-and-After Proof

Show plenty of real, consented before-and-after results from actual patients.
Quantity and authenticity matter more than polish — a wall of genuine
transformations does more than one perfect render. Always secure written consent,
avoid retouching, and pair images with honest context (timeframe, program) so the
proof stays credible and never tips into over-promising. This is the visual
counterpart to "evidence-led, emotion-aware."

## 5. Lots of Social Proof

Stack reviews, testimonials, and trust markers such as "#1 voted slimming clinic
in Malta" alongside star ratings and quotes from named patients (with permission).
Prioritise testimonials that speak to how life *feels* — energy, confidence,
climbing stairs, wearing the dress — not just kilos lost, mirroring the brand
voice. The more independent and specific the proof, the more it reassures a reader
who has "started over more times than she can count."

## 6. Link to the Item Page for Checkout

Give a single, obvious path to purchase or book. The primary CTA should lead
directly to the item / checkout page (or booking flow) with clear, calm wording
like "Start Your Plan" or "Book Your Consultation" — never aggressive countdowns
or fear-based urgency. Repeat the CTA at logical points (after the video, after
proof, at the foot of the page) so a ready-to-act visitor never has to scroll
back up to find it.

---

## Page Skeleton (Top to Bottom)

1. **H1 — Offer name** (with "Malta" where natural) + supporting subhead
2. **Primary CTA** (above the fold) → links to checkout / booking
3. **Practitioner video** — doctor-led explanation of the package
4. **Package contents** — clear list of what's included
5. **Before-and-after proof** — gallery of real, consented results
6. **Social proof** — reviews, "#1 voted slimming clinic in Malta", testimonials
7. **Reassurance / objection-handling** — safety, supervision, "you can live with it"
8. **Repeated CTA** → links to the item page for checkout
